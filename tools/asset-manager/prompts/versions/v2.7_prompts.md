# v2.7 prompts — B4-Lean → B1+ switch (ink-wash fusion preference)

**Date**: 2026-04-18
**Scope**: replace the four portrait-related keys in `tools/asset-manager/backend/templates.json`.
**Decision**: user preference for the wuxia watercolor + flying-white ink-wash fusion look
(tested in the B1 arm of the Phase-A sweep) over the "semi-realistic painterly"
direction that was cut in at v2.6 (B4-Lean).
**No backward-compat guarantees.** Product has no users.

## 1. What landed in production

Four keys in `tools/asset-manager/backend/templates.json` were replaced.
`item_template` and `scene_template` are untouched.

| key | change |
| --- | --- |
| `style_base`        | B4-Lean "Semi-realistic … painterly rendering …" → B1-family "Modern wuxia … ink-wash accents, crisp watercolor body, flying-white on garments" |
| `no_text_constraint` | **kept B4-Lean one-liner**: "No text, letters, calligraphy, seals, stamps, watermarks, or signatures anywhere in the image." |
| `style_negative`    | re-added the paper-texture-defense cluster from B1 (aged paper / ivory / cream / paper texture / rice paper / classical Chinese painting yellowing / gongbi-on-silk / heavy ink saturation / dense ink wash) while **keeping B4-Lean's eye/face negatives** (blurry eyes, smeared features, muddy face, flat/cel-shading). Dropped "muddy dark palette" (redundant with warm-cast / dim / gloomy block). |
| `portrait_template` | **kept B4-Lean's structural skeleton** (no pose+palette boilerplate, explicit `FACE AND EYES` block, faithfulness line). **Reinjected B1's ink DNA** by adding one fusion sentence before the composition line: *"The body itself is rendered in clean crisp watercolor, while a few airy flying-white ink splashes curl off the garments or trail into the clean negative space as atmospheric accents only."* Also swapped "jianghu wuxia character portrait" → "wuxia character portrait" to match B1 wording. |

## 2. Why B1+ instead of straight B1 or straight B4-Lean

### 2.1 Why not straight B1

B1 (`prompts/experiments/v2_B1_templates.json`) has ~294 words across style_base +
no_text + portrait_template. A lot of it is redundant:

- `no_text_constraint` repeats "text, letters, words, characters, writing systems,
  calligraphy, seals, stamps, chop marks, red seal marks, watermarks, signatures,
  inscriptions, or labels" — the B4-Lean one-liner proved equally effective in the
  v2.5 eval and costs 65 fewer tokens per call.
- `portrait_template` carries boilerplate about "poised, story-driven martial arts
  pose", "each character has their own distinct color palette", "Transparent
  background, nothing else behind the character" — the first two are already
  encoded by the v3 slot description (`pose` / `costume` slots), the third is
  already encoded in `style_base` and in the composition line.
- B1 has **no** `FACE AND EYES` block. In the Phase-A eval this showed as occasionally
  blurred or muddy faces — exactly what the B4-Lean FACE-AND-EYES clause was
  designed to fix.

### 2.2 Why not stay on B4-Lean (the v2.6 prod template)

- User preference: wants the watercolor + flying-white ink-wash fusion look from
  B1, not the semi-realistic painterly look with rim/volumetric lighting from
  B4-Lean. The latter was reading "too cinematic / too photorealistic" for a
  stylized wuxia card-art direction.
- B4-Lean's `style_negative` is missing the paper-texture defense cluster
  (aged paper / cream background / rice paper look / classical Chinese painting
  yellowing). Without it, gpt-image-1 sometimes regresses into sepia / ivory
  canvas rendering on wuxia-flavored prompts. B1 has this defense; B1+ keeps it.

### 2.3 Net result

- **Style DNA** comes from B1 (`style_base` + ink-wash line in template + paper-
  defense negatives).
- **Precision & eye clarity** come from B4-Lean (one-liner no-text, FACE-AND-EYES
  block, pose-boilerplate removal, eye/face negatives).
